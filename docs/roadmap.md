# Public Roadmap

MindCanary follows a narrow rule: every feature should help people understand
how their chosen routines change over time. It will not become an everything
app, productivity game, social network, or clinical control panel.

## Current Linux Alpha

- local encrypted history;
- optional check-ins and annotations;
- optional aggregate Chrome and GNOME/X11 context;
- descriptive sustained-window baseline comparisons;
- export, encrypted backup and restore, deletion, and local removal; and
- no account, telemetry, cloud dependency, or AI-controlled scoring.

## Next

- validate installation and comprehension with a small Linux cohort;
- measure usefulness before and after baseline readiness;
- identify tracking fatigue, alarming language, and privacy confusion;
- improve onboarding, backup file selection, and recovery-secret handling; and
- stabilize the optional browser distribution path.

## Later Candidates

- additional desktop operating systems;
- Firefox validation;
- narrow local integrations selected through user research;
- user-selected schedule-aware baseline groupings; and
- improved user-controlled exports for professional review.

Candidates are not commitments. New connectors must justify their permission
cost, reliability burden, and privacy impact.

## Out Of Scope

- diagnosis or clinical phase prediction;
- emergency monitoring or caregiver alerts;
- productivity scores, streaks, rankings, or guilt notifications;
- hidden profiling; and
- LLM-controlled feature extraction, thresholds, or insight state.
